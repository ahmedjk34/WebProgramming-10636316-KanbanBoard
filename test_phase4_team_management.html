üß™ Testing Instructions

Step 1: Access the Main Application

Open index.html in your browser and ensure you're logged in.

Expected: The application loads with the workspace type tabs (Personal/Teams) visible in the header.

Step 2: Switch to Teams Workspace

Click on the "Teams" tab in the header to switch to team workspace mode.

Expected:
  • Teams tab becomes active
  • Team controls appear in the header (Create Team, Manage Teams buttons)
  • Sidebar updates to show team-related sections

Step 3: Test Create Team Dialog

Click the "Create Team" button in the header.

Expected:
  • Create Team dialog opens
  • Form fields are present (Name, Description, Visibility)
  • Form validation works (required fields)
  • Dialog can be closed with Cancel button or X

Step 4: Test Team Management Dialog

Click the "Manage Teams" button in the header.

Expected:
  • Team Management dialog opens with three tabs
  • Teams tab shows empty state or existing teams
  • Tab switching works correctly
  • Create Team button is available in the dialog

Step 5: Test Team Creation

Fill out the Create Team form and submit it.

Expected:
  • Form submission works
  • Success message appears
  • Dialog closes automatically
  • Team appears in the teams list (if backend is working)

Step 6: Test Team Editing

In the Teams tab, click the edit button (‚úèÔ∏è) on a team card.

Expected:
  • Edit Team dialog opens
  • Form is pre-populated with team data
  • Changes can be saved

Step 7: Test Member Management

Switch to the "Members" tab in the Team Management dialog.

Expected:
  • Members list is displayed (with mock data)
  • Invite Member button is available
  • Member roles are shown (Admin/Member)
  • Remove member functionality works

Step 8: Test Workspace Management

Switch to the "Workspaces" tab in the Team Management dialog.

Expected:
  • Team workspaces list is displayed (with mock data)
  • Create Workspace button is available
  • Workspace actions (Open/Delete) are functional

Step 9: Test Responsive Design

Resize the browser window to test mobile responsiveness.

Expected:
  • Dialogs adapt to smaller screens
  • Team cards stack vertically on mobile
  • Buttons and controls remain accessible
